5 U.S. Code 

§ 5756 - Home marketing
incentive payment
(a) Under regulations prescribed under subsection (b), an agency may pay to an
employee who transfers in the interest of the Government an amount to
encourage the employee to aggressively market the employee’s residence at the
official station from which transferred when—
(1) the residence is entered into a relocation services program established
under a contract in accordance with section 5724c of this title to arrange for
the purchase of the residence;

(2) the employee finds a buyer who completes the purchase of the residence
through the program; and
(3) the sale of the residence results in a reduced cost to the Government.
(b)
(1) The Administrator of General Services shall prescribe regulations to carry
out this section.
(2) The regulations shall include a limitation on the maximum amount
payable with respect to an employee’s residence. The Administrator shall
establish the limitation in consultation with the Director of the Office of
Management and Budget. For fiscal years 1997 and 1998, the maximum
amount shall be the amount equal to five percent of the sale price of the
residence.
